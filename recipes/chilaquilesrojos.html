Chilaquiles

Chilaquiles are a delicious Mexican dish typically served for breakfast, although depending on personal preference, they can also be enjoyed as part of lunch. As a traditional and iconic food, chilaquiles can be prepared in many different ways. The mission is to share one of my favorite variations with you, so you can taste a little piece of Mexico. Today, you'll have on your hands the best way to prepare a majestic plate of chilaquiles rojos made with guajillo chili.

Servings: 2

Ingredients

  • 1 Tomato
  • 100 gr. roasted guajillo chili
  • 6 corn tortillas chopped on squares or triangles
  • 8 roasted garlic cloves
  • 1 cup of water
  • Crumbled queso fresco
  • Oil
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. In a pan with oil, fry the tortillas until they are golden and crispy. Then drain them and set them aside to continue with the preparation.
  2. In a blender, combine the chiles, tomato, and garlic, and blend until you get a smooth sauce.
  3. In the same pan where the tortillas were fried (with just a little oil), add the sauce and saut√© it for a few minutes.
  4. Add the water and salt to taste, and let it simmer for a bit.
  5. Once it starts to boil, add the fried tortillas and stir for a few minutes until the flavors concentrate
  6. Let the tortillas absorb some of the sauce's moisture and soften a little.
  7. Once the chilaquiles are ready, serve them on a plate and top them with crumbled queso fresco for an extra burst of flavor.

NOTES!!

Some more optional topings for an extra extra burst of flavor are: Onion slices, sour cream, sunny side up egg, shredded chicken, and more!
